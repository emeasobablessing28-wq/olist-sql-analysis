# Olist-SQL-Project
SQL analysis of an e-commerce dataset (Olist) focusing on revenue, product performance, delivery efficiency, and customer satisfaction.
## Tools Used
- SQLite (DB Browser)
- SQL

**KEY INSIGHTS**

IN SUMMERIES;

**Revenue Analysis:**
Monthly revenue and order volume exhibit an overall upward trend, reflecting growth in platform usage over time. Short-term fluctuations are observed, with decreases in revenue closely mirroring drops in order volume, indicating that revenue is largely driven by transaction count. Notably, a sharp decline in the final months (September–October 2018) appears to be due to incomplete data rather than a true drop in business activity

**Delivery performance Analysis:**
Analysis reveals a strong inverse relationship between delivery time and customer satisfaction. Orders with lower review scores experience significantly longer delivery times, with 1-star reviews averaging over 21 days compared to approximately 11 days for 5-star reviews. With an overall average delivery time of 12 days and about 8% of orders delivered late, the findings suggest that delivery speed is a key driver of customer satisfaction, and delays have a measurable negative impact on reviews

**Products/Categories analysis:**
Revenue is concentrated within a small group of product categories, with Health & Beauty and Watches & Gifts emerging as the top revenue contributors. While Bed, Bath & Table records the highest number of items sold, its revenue is comparatively lower, indicating a lower average price per item. In contrast, Watches & Gifts generates high revenue with fewer sales, suggesting higher-value transactions. This highlights distinct revenue drivers across categories, with some driven by volume and others by pricing.

**Overall Summery:**
The analysis reveals that Olist’s performance is driven by three interconnected factors: transaction volume, product category structure, and delivery efficiency. Revenue growth is primarily driven by order volume, while product categories differ significantly between high-volume low-value segments and low-volume high-value segments. Additionally, delivery speed plays a critical role in customer satisfaction, with delays strongly associated with lower review scores.
